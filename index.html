I'm PhD in ecosystem ecology with experimental research testing the effect of climate changes on the microbial community and the nutrient cycle. Former member of Aquatic Ecology lab of Federal University of Rio de Janeiro and from the Bromeliad Working Group of the University of British Columbia, currently I'm researcher and developer at Lana where I lead projects centered on free/open source technology projects applied to ecosystem ecology, participative science and technology. My areas of interest are environmental changes, social ecology, anthropocene, and free and open technology.

fractaL

An Eco-Art project using synesthetic experience as the base for digital literacy and ecological understanding. The environmental data under the calculable parameters fits perfectly on the machines logic and constraints imagination. In fractaL the uncountable is prioritized to stimulate creative processes and enhance knowledge dynamics.

LAIAdb

LAIA is a friend-to-friend network (F2F), which only allows access to the data by trusted nodes on the network. The project aims to improve collaborative research between groups while protecting both research groups and their data against censorship, persecution and other forms of political retaliation.

Climobike

A mobile monitoring system focused on collaborative environmental data sampling. A device adaptable to different models of bicycle and capable to store and share real-time georefered data about temperature, humidity and the air quality.

BALCAR

A long-term research on the emission of greenhouse gases (GHG) by hydroelectric reservoir in different regions of Brazil looking for trends and patterns on the biotic and abiotic aspects of these artificial ecosystems and their potential as GHG emissors
